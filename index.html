Life Cycle of a Magic the Gathering Player

• Life Cycle of a Magic The Gathering Player •

  • Your First Pack of Cards
  • Building a Deck
  • Learning to Play
  • Getting Addicted to Tournaments
  • Setting Money on Fire

    • • Your First Pack of Cards •

    You get your first pack of cards. You're excited! You open the celephane wrapper and peel through the cardboard. You got a mythic rare! Your heart races. Your friends cheer!

    You buy 50 MORE PACKS!

    • Building a Deck •

    You have bought 150 packs of Magic Cards this week. Your collection is large and takes up most area within your home. You finally decide to build a deck so you can play the game you've invested your rent check into.

    • Learning to Play •

    You play two tutorials on MTGArena and feel confident in your abilities. You meet your friends at a Denny's. It's 8pm. You play till sun up. You still havent won a game but your friends assure you you're ready for a tournament.

    Time to spend $60 to enter a tournament!

    • Getting Addicted to Tournaments •

    You've played five real games of magic. It's 6am and you drive in your friend's mom's minivan 2 hours away to a random card store you didn't know existed. You play 8 rounds of magic for 9 hours, and only beat little Suzie in your last round who went 0-8. A win is a win though and you are already jazzed to enter next weeks tournament.

    • Setting Money on Fire •

    Magic comes out with a new set every three weeks. You buy as many packs as you can afford of each new set to stay relevant. It would be cheaper to have a drug problem at this point. Your friends are all magic players. Your significant other is a magic player. you all share a house made from boxes of open magic cards. This is life now.
